History

Famous Player

  • Shane Horgan

The most famous player to come out of Boyne RFC is Shane Horgan.Born in Meath in 1978 Shane always had a passion for rugby,although blossiming in GAA for Meath his desire was to play rugby.He started his career at Boyne RFC and from his first few weeks at the club everybody knew he would not be here for long.

When he finished School he was signed up by Lansdowne.A year later Leinster saw the ability of this young man and signed him straight to the first team.He made his debut for leinster in 1998 and offically became a leinster legend.

Drogheda and Delvin

Before offically becoming Boyne RFC in 1998, Drogheda and Delvin amalgamted to become one club.In youth rugby Drogheda and Delvin were very successful in underage rugby winning many trophies.

  • Leinster Junior cup
  • The Eddie Maher Invitational
  • The All Ireland Championship

Although Drogheda and Delvin's youth teams were very successful the senior team were not. Many youth players moved on to senior level and enhanced there playing level with other clubs.

In 1997 the discussion began about the amalgamtion of the two clubs.With a lot of attempts to disband each club respectively were rejected, a deal was agreed to form a new team Boyne RFC.
